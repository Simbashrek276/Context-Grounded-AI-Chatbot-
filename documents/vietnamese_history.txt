Vietnamese history spans thousands of years, marked by resilience and cultural development.

The ancient kingdom of Van Lang, established around the 7th century BCE, is considered one of the earliest Vietnamese states. It was ruled by the Hung Kings, who are regarded as the founding monarchs of the Vietnamese nation.

Vietnam experienced Chinese domination for nearly a millennium, from 111 BCE to 939 CE. This long period ended when the Battle of Bach Dang River in 938 CE led to independence under the general Ngo Quyen, who defeated the Southern Han fleet using wooden stakes hidden beneath the water.

The Ly, Tran, and Le dynasties later shaped a golden era of Vietnamese civilisation, with advancements in agriculture, education, and law. The Temple of Literature, Vietnam's first national university, was established in 1070 during the Ly dynasty. The Tran dynasty is especially remembered for repelling three Mongol invasions in the 13th century.

In the 19th century, Vietnam faced French colonization. French Indochina was consolidated in the 1880s, and colonial rule lasted until 1945.

The 20th century saw the rise of Ho Chi Minh and the struggle for independence. This struggle culminated in the Vietnam War, which ended in 1975 with the reunification of North and South Vietnam under a single government.
