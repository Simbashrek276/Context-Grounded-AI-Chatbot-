Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants make their own food using sunlight.

It takes place mainly in the leaves, inside tiny structures called chloroplasts.

Chloroplasts contain a green pigment called chlorophyll, which absorbs light energy from the Sun.

During photosynthesis, plants take in carbon dioxide from the air and water from the soil.

Using light energy, the plant converts these into glucose, a sugar that stores energy, and releases oxygen as a by-product.

Because it releases oxygen and removes carbon dioxide, photosynthesis is essential for life on Earth.
